### What this document is and isn't

These terms govern paid sponsored-content engagements between Amazon and individual creators. They are NOT the Amazon Associates program (the affiliate-link program where you earn commission on sales generated by your links), which operates under separate terms. The Creator Program is for direct contracts where Amazon engages a creator to produce specific content for a campaign.

A creator typically signs these terms by accepting an Agreement (a booking confirmation or insertion order) that incorporates this Terms & Conditions document by reference.

### Content requirements and review

Every piece of content has to go through Amazon review before it goes public:

- **Submission timing:** At least 10 business days before scheduled publication. Amazon's review and approval is required, though the terms say approval "will not be unreasonably withheld."
- **Disclosures:** `#ad` or `#sponsored` must be the FIRST tag on every piece of content. Plus full compliance with the FTC's Guides Concerning the Use of Endorsements and Testimonials in Advertising.
- **Tagging restrictions:** No tagging other brands or individuals without Amazon's prior written approval.
- **Audience restrictions:** Content must be directed at adults (18+), unless specifically engaged for the Amazon Teens experience (in which case content is directed at teens 13+).
- **Children in content:** Children who are or appear to be under 3 cannot appear. Under 13 generally cannot appear without prior Amazon approval. If Amazon devices are shown with children, specific setup conditions apply.

If Amazon provides a "Brief" outlining the campaign direction, all content must conform to it. If it doesn't, the content doesn't count as completed and a conforming replacement is required.

### The license you grant Amazon

This is the most consequential clause for many creators. The license is:

- **Exclusive.** You can't grant the same rights to anyone else for the same content.
- **Irrevocable.** You can't take it back.
- **Worldwide.**
- **Royalty-free.** No additional compensation beyond what the Agreement specifies.
- **Perpetual.** Forever.
- **Sublicensable / assignable.** Amazon can give the rights to third parties.

Amazon can use the content, your name, your photograph, and your likeness. They can modify the content (with the qualifier that modifications can't materially change the meaning), translate it, distribute it, republish it, and create derivative works.

This grant survives termination of the Agreement. Ending the relationship with Amazon does not end Amazon's rights to use what you produced for them.

### Prohibited content

Beyond the audience and disclosure rules, content cannot include:

- Images of minors without proper authorization, or anyone else without proper authorization
- Political materials
- Software viruses
- Links to external (non-Amazon) websites
- Solicitations for products you weren't engaged to promote
- Anything that infringes someone else's IP rights
- Anything illegal, offensive, threatening, defamatory, or that Amazon deems objectionable in its sole discretion
- Nudity

### Payment

Net 60 days after Amazon receives and accepts your content, services, and invoice. Creators are responsible for any taxes, agency fees, or withholdings. Amazon may deduct or withhold any taxes it is legally obligated to. If Amazon sends physical materials (clothing, accessories) that don't have to be returned, title passes to you when the items are handed to the carrier.

### Termination

Amazon can terminate the agreement either:

- **For any reason** with 10 days' prior written notice, or
- **Immediately** for any breach of the terms.

On termination, the creator must:

- Stop using Amazon Materials in all media
- Not publish any new materials containing the content or Amazon Materials
- Refund any payments for content or services if the termination was tied to harm to Amazon's or the creator's reputation

After termination, Amazon's only payment obligation is for services already performed and liabilities already incurred.

### Confidentiality

The terms of the ITC and any specific Agreement are Amazon's confidential information. Creators can't disclose them without prior written consent, except to business representatives or where legally required. Confidentiality survives termination.

### Disputes, arbitration, and class action waiver

Unlike regular Amazon consumer disputes (which the Conditions of Use route to King County, Washington courts), creator disputes are sent to binding arbitration through the American Arbitration Association under its Commercial Arbitration Rules. Key points:

- **Before filing arbitration**, creators must send a letter to Amazon's registered agent describing the claim
- **Arbitration is individual only.** Class, consolidated, and representative actions are all waived.
- **Court is allowed only as a fallback** if for some reason a claim proceeds in court rather than arbitration, in which case it goes to state or federal court in King County, Washington, with jury trial waived
- **Injunctive relief in court is allowed** for IP infringement claims, notwithstanding the arbitration requirement

The Federal Arbitration Act, federal arbitration law, and Washington state law apply.

### Amazon's liability cap on creator claims

If Amazon is found liable to a creator for direct damages, the cap is the compensation Amazon paid that creator in the 12-month period preceding the claim. Indirect, incidental, consequential, special, punitive, exemplary, and reliance damages are all excluded, regardless of cause or theory.

### Updates to the terms

Amazon can amend the ITC at any time by posting the updated version, with or without notice. Continued engagement as a creator after an update counts as acceptance of the amended terms.
