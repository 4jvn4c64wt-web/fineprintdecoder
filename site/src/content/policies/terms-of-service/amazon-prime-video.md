### What this document is

Prime Video's Terms of Use sit on top of Amazon's broader Conditions of Use. The Prime Video terms cover the things specific to streaming and digital video: what counts as Subscription, Rental, Purchased, PPV, and Free content; what devices you can use; geographic limits; cancellation rules for digital transactions; and the licensing terms for video content. Anything not covered here (governing law, dispute resolution, account-level termination of your whole Amazon account) is governed by Amazon's Conditions of Use, which is a separate document.

### The five categories of content

Prime Video offers content in five different transactional models, and the rules differ for each:

- **Subscription Digital Content.** Available while your subscription is active (Prime, a standalone Prime Video subscription, or a third-party subscription accessed through Prime Video). Goes away when the subscription ends.
- **Rental Digital Content.** A time-limited license. You can usually watch within a viewing period defined in the Usage Rules.
- **Purchased Digital Content.** Indefinite access, but explicitly not permanent. Amazon's terms allow purchased content to become unavailable if Amazon loses the underlying licensing, and Amazon disclaims liability for that.
- **PPV Digital Content.** Pay-per-view for a limited window, typically tied to a live event or premiere.
- **Free Digital Content.** Ad-supported or promotional access, time-limited.

The license you get for any of these is "non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable, limited." You're licensed to watch, not to redistribute.

### Cancellation rules in detail

Cancellation rules apply to two different things on Prime Video, and they don't work the same way:

**Subscription cancellation.** If Prime Video access comes from Amazon Prime, cancel through Prime (see our [Prime cancellation summary](/cancellation-policies/amazon-prime/)). If you have a standalone, video-only subscription billed directly by Amazon, cancel through Your Account; refund rules mirror Prime's (full refund within 3 business days of signing up, or full refund later only if you haven't accessed any video-only subscription content since the last charge). If you signed up through a third party, cancel through that third party.

**Rental and purchase cancellation.** A 48-hour cancellation window applies from the time of purchase (14 days in the UK and EU). This window dies the moment you start watching or downloading. Pre-orders are cancellable until the release date. PPV transactions are cancellable until the scheduled start.

### Geographic and VPN restrictions

Prime Video is only available in specific countries, and the available content varies by country due to licensing. Amazon uses geolocation technology to verify your location, and the terms explicitly prohibit using any technology to obscure or disguise your location (i.e., VPNs). Violating this is a contract breach that can trigger account termination.

### Software liability cap is unusually low

Most consumer SaaS limitation-of-liability clauses cap at the fees paid in the past 12 months. Prime Video has a second, much lower cap that applies specifically to software-related claims: $50 total. If the Prime Video app crashes, corrupts data, fails to play content you paid for, or causes any other software-related loss, Amazon's total contractual exposure for those claims is $50. Content-related claims (e.g., paid-for content becoming unavailable) use the more standard 12-month-fees cap.

### Service modifications and discontinuation

Amazon reserves the right to modify or discontinue Prime Video at any time without notice, except where local law requires advance notice. EU and UK customers get 30 days' notice for "Material Changes" that adversely affect usability, and can cancel within 30 days after a material change takes effect for a pro-rated refund.

### Communications and marketing

Subscribers consent to receive electronic communications from Amazon, including marketing emails, push notifications, and Amazon Message Center posts. UK, EU, Turkey, and Brazil customers opt in separately. Marketing communications can be turned off in account settings; transactional communications (billing, password resets, content availability) cannot.

### What Amazon can do with your viewing data

Amazon collects what you watch, when, and how. This data is governed by the Amazon Privacy Notice for your country. Amazon may share viewing information with content providers (including third-party subscription services offered through Prime Video) in a non-identifiable form by default. Identifiable sharing only happens with your specific authorization for a given provider.

### Dispute resolution lives elsewhere

Disputes about Prime Video are governed by the dispute-resolution provisions in Amazon's Conditions of Use, not in this document. That means the governing law, jurisdiction, jury-trial waiver, and any arbitration or class-action terms come from the broader Amazon agreement.
