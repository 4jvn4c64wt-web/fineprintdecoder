### What qualifies for a return

Apple's standard window is 14 calendar days from receipt — not from purchase, and not from shipping. The clock starts when you have the product in hand. The item must be in its original condition with everything that came in the box: cords, adapters, documentation.

A short list of items is permanently ineligible regardless of condition: opened software (including software that came with a printed license and has a broken seal), electronic software downloads, Software Up-to-Date upgrade products, Apple Gift Cards, and Apple Developer products like memberships and WWDC tickets.

### Personalized and custom-configured items

Engraved products — AirPods, iPads with laser engraving — and Macs that were custom-configured at order cannot be exchanged in-store. They can be returned (for a refund), but not swapped for a different model or configuration at a retail location. ZEISS Optical Inserts – Prescription are a special case: they're custom third-party products and cannot be returned at an Apple Store at all; returns go through Apple directly within 14 days.

### Gift returns

Gift recipients use the Gift Returns page (apple.com) with either the product serial number or the order number plus the UPC from the packaging. The refund comes back as an electronic Apple Gift Card, not cash. If you'd prefer a physical Gift Card, you need to call Apple at 1-800-MY-APPLE.

### How refunds are issued

Refunds go back to the original payment method. Credit and debit card refunds post within five business days of Apple receiving the item — the bank, not Apple, controls when it shows on your statement. Apple Gift Card purchases are refunded as a new Apple Gift Card by email. Apple Account balance payments are refunded to your balance (or as a Gift Card if your balance is near the maximum).

### Price protection

If Apple lowers the price on an Apple-branded product within 14 calendar days of when you received it, you can request a refund of the difference. Contact an Apple Store or Retail Customer Care (1-800-MY-APPLE) within 14 days of the price change. This doesn't apply to limited-time sales like Black Friday or other promotional events. The cap is 10 units per product.

### Puerto Rico

Shipping an item worth more than $2,500 from Puerto Rico back to the continental US requires filing an Export Declaration through ACE (ace.cbp.dhs.gov) before initiating the return. Apple's returns page has the commodity code lookup link for this step.
